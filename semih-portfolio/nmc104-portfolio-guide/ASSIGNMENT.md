# NMC104 – Portfolio Website Assignment Brief

## What you are building

A personal portfolio website that represents you as a media and communication student. This is a frontend-only project, meaning it is a website made of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript files — no backend, no server, no database.

The site lives on your computer. You will submit the files, not a live URL.

---

## Required sections

Your portfolio must include all of the following:

**1. Introduction / About**
A short section that introduces who you are. Your name, your department, what you are interested in. Keep it honest and personal — this is not a corporate bio.

**2. Work / Projects**
At least 3 pieces of work. These can be anything you have produced: a photo, a design from NMC104, a video, a poster, a social media project, a written piece. If you do not have 3 yet, include work in progress or briefly describe an idea you want to pursue.

**3. Contact**
A way for someone to reach you. An email address or a link to your social media profile is enough.

---

## Design requirements

Your site must show intentional design decisions. It is not enough to accept the first output Antigravity gives you. You are expected to:

- Choose a color palette (use Coolors: [coolors.co](https://coolors.co/))
- Choose typography (fonts) that fit your personal style
- Have a consistent visual language across all sections

Look at [component.gallery](https://component.gallery/) to understand common UI patterns (navigation bars, cards, hero sections, footers). You do not have to copy them — use them as reference to understand what these elements are called and how they work.

---

## What you are graded on

The grading rubric from the midterm applies here too (5 criteria × 20 points = 100 points):

| Criterion | What it means |
|---|---|
| Visual Hierarchy & Composition | Is the layout clear? Does the eye know where to look first? |
| Typography | Do the font choices make sense? Are they readable? |
| Color & Visual Consistency | Does the color palette hold together across the whole site? |
| Concept & Originality | Does the site feel like *you*, or does it feel generic? |
| Technical Craft & File Quality | Are the files clean and working? Does the site open correctly in a browser? |

---

## A rule that is non-negotiable

Your site may only contain your own work. No stock images, no stock illustrations, no photos from the internet. Every image on your site must be something you made or photographed yourself. Every piece of text must be your own writing.

If your site contains stock visuals, it will not be graded.

---

## What you do NOT need to do

- You do not need to publish the site online
- You do not need to write any code yourself
- You do not need to use a framework (React, Vue, etc.) — plain HTML/CSS is fine
- You do not need to include every project you have ever made — 3 is enough
